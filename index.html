Meet the Trainers

Caitlin has always been passionate in her love of animals as well as her desire to understand and work with them. She grew up surrounded by pets, but her interest in dog training was ignited in high school by a blind teacher who relied upon a Seeing Eye dog. She soon began volunteering with The Seeing Eye, raising and training two puppies from 8-weeks old to a year and a half before sending them back to join the program to become official guide dogs.
At Franklin & Marshall College, Caitlin studied learning theory and animal cognition, earning a degree in Animal Behavior. For four years, she was the student manager of a research team and employed positive reinforcement and clicker training to teach capuchin monkeys to use touchscreen computers. The skills and principles she learned at F&M transferred seamlessly into Caitlin’s career as a professional dog trainer. Caitlin got her dog training certification through Animal Behavior College. She is also a member of the Association of Professional Dog Trainers. She firmly believes that all dogs should be treated with respect and dignity and that each one deserves a good, loving home.

Jessica grew up with animals and has been training them for as long as she can remember. She has successfully raised several puppies for The Seeing Eye and Canine Companions for Independence. Jessica is currently in the process of obtaining her masters degree in Animal Behavior and Conservation from Hunter College. She has worked with almost every issue, and met every type of dog. She welcomes the opportunity to train your dog to be the loving, well-mannered friend that you need and deserve. Additionally, Jessica's training style incorporates patience and positive reinforcement while still remaining highly effective.

As a dog owner and lover since early childhood Brian knew early on that working with dogs was his true passion. He began by apprenticing with a local kennel owner and trainer. Since then, he has continued working with dogs professionally for almost 10 years. Additionally, Brian has logged countless volunteer hours with numerous shelters and rescue organizations, helping to foster, rehabilitate and train abandoned dogs. He continues to study canine training and behavior independently and regularly attends continuing education seminars to keep current with the latest dog training methods and techniques.

Sean is a former biologist turned full-time dog trainer. She enjoys applying the teaching and problem solving skills she learned as a scientist to solving dog training and behavior problems. She takes pride in helping owners meet their goals, whether it’s getting off to the best possible start with a new puppy or teaching a rambunctious rescue to walk down the street without barking at all the neighbors. Additionally, Sean is an approved evaluator for the American Kennel Club Canine Good Citizen Program and is also close to becoming a Certified Professional Dog Trainer (CPDT-KA).



Training Services Offered


Puppy Orientation

Our specialized Puppy Orientation group classes offer flexible scheduling, small classes and ample off-leash playtime for your young puppy. This class covers important topics like: socialization, handling, house-training, nipping, puppy play and leash greetings. (Limited to 6 puppies per class)


Basic Obedience

With our play and train style, dogs enrolled in basic obedience learn the foundation of reliable commands like sit, down, leave it, coming when called and walking on a leash. Our trainers will work one-on-one with you and your dog in a setting that is comfortable and conducive to successful learning.


Advanced Obedience

Advanced training will help you to teach your dog to listen to you all the time, whether or not there is a leash attached. We will start with a long line before eventually going on with off leash training. Both you and your will be so much happier (and safer!) with a higher level of obedience in your toolbox of tricks.


Behavorial Concerns

This covers a variety of issues such as chewing, digging and barking and the more serious issues such as resource guarding, dog-to-dog or dog-to-human aggression, separation anxiety, fear and handling issues. We will work with you and your dog to achieve the best possible outcome.
